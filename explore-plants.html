Plant Nutrition Library

Explore Plants

Discover the healing power of whole-food plants — evidence-based nutritional profiles for the herbs, vegetables, seeds, and fruits that support lasting health.

Food is information for your body. Each plant below carries a unique combination of nutrients, phytochemicals, and fibre that works synergistically to support your health goals. Use this library alongside our nutrition guides or book a personalised consultation to learn how to incorporate them into your daily routine.

Leafy Green
Spinach
Spinacia oleracea

A nutrient powerhouse rich in iron, folate, and magnesium. Spinach supports red blood cell production, energy metabolism, and bone health. Its nitrates may also improve cardiovascular efficiency.

Iron Folate Magnesium Vitamin K
Herb & Spice
Turmeric
Curcuma longa

Celebrated for curcumin — its potent anti-inflammatory compound. Regular use may reduce markers of chronic inflammation, support joint health, and protect against oxidative stress. Best absorbed with black pepper.

Curcumin Manganese Anti-inflammatory
Fruit & Berry
Blueberries
Vaccinium corymbosum

Among the highest antioxidant fruits available, blueberries are rich in anthocyanins that support brain health, reduce oxidative stress, and may improve insulin sensitivity and memory function.

Anthocyanins Vitamin C Fibre Antioxidants
Root
Ginger
Zingiber officinale

A well-researched digestive aid, ginger contains gingerols and shogaols that reduce nausea, support gut motility, and have powerful anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Gingerols Potassium Digestive Health
Leafy Green
Kale
Brassica oleracea var. sabellica

One of the most nutrient-dense foods on Earth. Kale delivers exceptional levels of Vitamins K, C, and A, alongside calcium and sulforaphane — a compound with notable anti-cancer research support.

Vitamin K Vitamin C Sulforaphane Calcium
Root & Bulb
Garlic
Allium sativum

Allicin — released when garlic is crushed — has potent antimicrobial and immune-boosting effects. Regular consumption is associated with reduced blood pressure and improved cholesterol profiles.

Allicin Vitamin B6 Immune Support
Seed
Chia Seeds
Salvia hispanica

Tiny but mighty, chia seeds are packed with plant-based omega-3 fatty acids, soluble fibre, and complete protein. They form a gel when soaked, slowing digestion and supporting sustained energy and blood sugar balance.

Omega-3 ALA Fibre Calcium Complete Protein
Leafy Green
Moringa
Moringa oleifera

Called the "miracle tree," moringa leaves contain more protein per gram than many animal sources, alongside high concentrations of iron, calcium, and vitamins A and C. Used widely to address nutritional deficiencies.

Protein Iron Vitamin A Isothiocyanates
Seed
Flaxseeds
Linum usitatissimum

An outstanding source of alpha-linolenic acid (ALA omega-3), lignans, and soluble fibre. Ground flaxseeds support hormone balance, bowel regularity, and healthy LDL cholesterol levels.

ALA Omega-3 Lignans Soluble Fibre Hormone Balance
