Plants as Partners in Your Health

Every plant on your plate carries something unique — a quiet gift of nourishment, protection, or warmth. These profiles explore three remarkable plants from a wholistic view: not just what nutrients they contain, but how they support your body, your energy, and your daily wellbeing.

🌿
Leafy Green · Wild Herb

Purslane

Portulaca oleracea

Purslane is a humble, resilient plant that grows freely in gardens and fields across the world. Its small, fleshy leaves carry a mild, pleasantly sour flavour — and beneath that simplicity lies remarkable nourishment. Among all the vegetables you might add to your plate, few are as rich in heart- and mind-supporting healthy fats. Used for centuries in Middle Eastern, Mediterranean, and Asian kitchens, purslane is a quiet powerhouse that deserves a place in everyday eating.

What Purslane Supports

Heart & Mind Nourishment One of the richest plant sources of omega-3 healthy fats — the same fats that support a calm heart, clear mind, and balanced mood.
Natural Hydration Its fleshy, succulent leaves hold water and minerals that gently support hydration from the inside out.
Bone & Muscle Support Rich in calcium and magnesium — minerals that work together to keep bones strong and muscles relaxed and well-functioning.
Protective Plant Goodness Contains a wealth of protective pigments and plant compounds that help shield your cells and keep your body in balance.
Light on the Body Very low in calories and naturally gentle on digestion — easy to add to meals without any heaviness.
Steady Energy Support Its B vitamins help your body convert food into energy smoothly, supporting a steady, calm vitality through the day.

How to Enjoy It

🥗 Raw in salads 🍳 Lightly sautéed 🫙 Added to soups & stews 🥚 Folded into scrambled eggs 🌮 In wraps & flatbreads 🧆 Classic in Middle Eastern dishes
🌱 A gentle note: Fresh purslane is best eaten promptly — its leaves soften quickly after picking. If you find it growing in your garden, harvest the tender tips and add them straight to your next meal.
🥬
Leafy Green · Salad Herb

Rocket

Eruca sativa · also known as Arugula

Rocket is a bold, peppery leafy green with centuries of history in Mediterranean kitchens. That distinctive bite and warmth you taste is a sign of its natural protective qualities — a gift from the plant itself. Beyond its lively flavour, rocket is deeply nourishing, offering support for your bones, blood, energy, and your body's own ability to stay resilient. It's one of those leaves that does a lot quietly, without fuss.

What Rocket Supports

Strong Bones A rich source of bone-building minerals and vitamin K, which work together to keep your skeleton strong and well-supported throughout life.
Healthy Blood & Energy High in folate — a vital nutrient that supports healthy blood cells, steady energy, and is especially important during pregnancy and growth.
Body's Natural Protection Rocket's peppery compounds help support your body's natural defences and ability to stay balanced and resilient day to day.
Vision & Skin Health Rich in beta-carotene, which the body converts to vitamin A — important for healthy eyes, glowing skin, and a strong immune system.
Gentle Digestive Support Its mild bitterness stimulates digestive juices naturally, making it a traditional after-meal leaf in many Mediterranean cultures.
Light & Hydrating Low in calories and naturally hydrating, rocket adds volume and flavour to meals without heaviness — perfect for everyday eating.

How to Enjoy It

🥗 Fresh salads & bowls 🍕 On warm pizza or flatbread 🍝 Tossed through warm pasta 🥪 In sandwiches & wraps 🍳 Wilted into eggs or stir-fries 🥣 Blended into pesto
💛 Flavour tip: The younger the leaf, the milder the bite. Baby rocket has a gentler flavour — wonderful for those new to it. Larger, older leaves are more peppery and pair beautifully with sweet or creamy ingredients.
🌰
Warming Spice · Bark

Cinnamon

Cinnamomum verum (Ceylon) & Cinnamomum cassia

Cinnamon is one of the world's oldest and most cherished spices — the dried inner bark of a tropical tree, rolled into the familiar quills you know. Its warm, sweet, slightly woody fragrance has perfumed kitchens and healing traditions across Asia, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean for thousands of years. Far beyond its beautiful flavour, cinnamon gently supports balanced energy, a settled digestion, and a calm, warm feeling in the body. A small daily pinch can make a meaningful difference.

What Cinnamon Supports

Balanced Blood Sugar & Energy One of cinnamon's most well-known gifts — it helps your body manage energy from food more steadily, supporting fewer highs and lows through the day.
Warm, Settled Digestion Its natural warming quality soothes the digestive system, easing bloating, settling discomfort, and encouraging smooth digestion after meals.
Protective Plant Richness Cinnamon is extraordinarily rich in protective plant compounds — gram for gram, it is among the most protective spices you can reach for daily.
Heart & Circulation Support Traditional and modern wisdom both recognise cinnamon's gentle support for healthy circulation and maintaining comfortable cholesterol levels.
Comforting & Grounding The warmth of cinnamon is more than flavour — its aroma and taste bring a sense of calm and grounding, especially welcome in cooler seasons.
Natural Antimicrobial Quality Traditionally used to support the body's resistance — cinnamon's natural compounds have long been valued for their protective and preserving properties.

How to Enjoy It

☕ In warming teas & lattes 🥣 Sprinkled on porridge & oats 🍌 In smoothies & banana nice-cream 🍞 Baked into breads & muffins 🫕 In slow-cooked tagines & stews 🍎 Roasted with apples or sweet potato
🍂 Choosing your cinnamon: There are two common types — Ceylon (true cinnamon) and Cassia. Ceylon is lighter, sweeter, and the preferred choice for everyday use in larger amounts. Cassia is stronger and more widely available. For daily cooking, both are wonderful in normal culinary amounts.
